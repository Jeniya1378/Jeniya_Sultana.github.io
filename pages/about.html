About

Professional Summary

I work on research that examines how data, sensing technologies, and intelligent systems operate in real-world, non-ideal environments. I’m particularly interested in using multimodal data to study safety, performance, and well-being in physically and cognitively demanding settings. Much of my work focuses on translating research ideas into methods and systems that can be evaluated outside the lab, where variability, noise, and human factors play a significant role. I enjoy working at the intersection of sensing, data analysis, and applied engineering, and thinking carefully about what can be measured reliably and used meaningfully in practice. More broadly, I value interdisciplinary research and enjoy collaborating across domains to develop technical solutions that are grounded in real constraints and designed for practical impact.
